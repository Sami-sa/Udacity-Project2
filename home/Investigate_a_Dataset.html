Introduction¶

In this project, I will be investigating through more than 100K appoinments recoreds to get better understaning of why 30% of patients miss thier medical appointments in Brazil. The appointment records include some characterisitcs about the appointmet, some about the patients, and about the medical center. Below, I will put some information about each of characterisitcs.

During the investigation, I try to answer the following questions to get better under standing and be able to share a conclusion of why 30% of patiens miss their appointment:

1- How many appointments were missed? And what was the pecentage of the missed appointments?

2- Which age group has the most and least appointment miss interms of numbers, and percentages?

3- How many appointments were missed given the patient receives the govermental financial aid? and

4- What is their percentage compared to the whole sample? and to the people missed their appointments?

other questions could be answered as well on later time:

1- what about other given illness? are their ratios more or less than who get the financial aid?

2- Do people with more than 1 illness tend to miss their appoint more often?

3- Which location has the most and least missings in terms of numbers and percentages?

4- Are those locations visted by patients receives financial aid? (locations with high poverty)

Conclusions¶

  • The data indicates that about 20% of 110,327 appointments were missed by 28% of 62190 unique pateints missed at least one appointment as shown below.

  • 8,133 patients scheduled appointment(s) and did not attend. That represent 13% of the total number of patients, and 46% of the patients who has missed at least one appoinement, and 24% of them are kids under 15 years old. Most likely would need their guardians to book for them the appointments and take them to visit the doctors.

  • I would suggest a close look into this category more than the others, to facilitate their visits. Maybe their parents could not get permissions to leave work, or something else.

  • The numbers show that the group age from 15 to 30 are has the most appointment missed, and the number decreased afterwards. On the other hand, in terms of percentage, the centenarians have the most missed appointments with minimum appointments as numbers. Most likely, they cannot be independent at this ages, and would need at least some organization to take care of them.

  • The second to most appointments missed in terms of percentage are the people with age group from 15 to 30. I would suggest increase the awarenece among this group of age and benifit from the social media in an awarenece campaign. This group or people are the gurdians or the near future guardians for the first group under 15 years old.

  • There are 2577 appointments missed given that the patients receives financial aid. They represent 2.3% from the who community, and 11.6% from the appointments missed. Those people seems to have high rate of commitment, maybe they have more flexability in terms of time and do not need prior permissions.

  • The team collected the data has done a great job; however, the analysis has faced some limitation such as:
    1. There is no clue for patients point of view behind missing appointments, especially for those who repeatedly did,
    2. There is field for connection between the patients, to verify whether the guardians how missed the appointments, also responsible for the same kids, or elders missed their apoointments,
    3. It would be great to have level of education included in the data, to check if there is a relation between it and missing the appointmrnts, and
    4. The distance between patients and midical centers, it would be important to pay attention to this factor and check if there is a considerable relation between it and missing the appointments.
